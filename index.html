Pro Tips

  • Always preheat your vessel to keep the brewing temperature stable.
  • Rinsing the paper filter removes any unwanted papery taste.
  • Freshly ground beans make the biggest difference in flavor!

How to Brew

Recipe 1: The Classic Pawr-Over

  1. The Prep: Heat your water to 205°F. Place the filter in the dripper and rinse it thoroughly with hot water. Dump the rinse water. Grind 20g of coffee to a medium-fine consistency and add it to the filter, giving it a gentle shake to level the bed.
  2. The Bloom: Start your timer and pour 40g of water, ensuring all the grounds are saturated. Wait for 45 seconds. You should see the coffee bed bubble and expand (carbon dioxide escaping).
  3. The Brew: Pour in slow, concentric circles starting from the center and moving outward. Pour in phases: up to 150g, let it drain slightly, then up to 250g, and finally up to your total weight of 320g.
  4. The Finish: The entire process should take about 3 minutes. Swirl the finished brew and enjoy.

Recipe 2: The Cool-Cat Cold Brew

  1. The Grind: Grind 100g of coffee very coarsely (like sea salt).
  2. The Mix: Add grounds to a pitcher. Pour 800g of cold or room-temperature water over the grounds.
  3. The Steep: Stir gently to ensure all grounds are wet. Cover and refrigerate for 16 to 24 hours.
  4. The Filter: Pour the mixture through a paper filter or fine mesh sieve to remove the grounds. Serve over ice!

Recipe 3: The Fluffy Feline French Press

  1. The Prep: Boil water and let it sit for a minute to reach about 200°F. Grind 30g of coffee coarsely.
  2. The Pour: Add grounds to the empty French press. Pour 500g of water directly over the grounds.
  3. The Steep: Set a timer for 4 minutes. Do not touch the plunger yet!
  4. The Plunge: After 4 minutes, gently break the top crust with a spoon. Place the lid on and slowly press the plunger down. Pour immediately.
